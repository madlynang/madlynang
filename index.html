Hi, I’m Maddie 🌿 I’m a Master’s student at Temple University working in ecology and biodiversity conservation. I’m especially interested in how landscapes, species, and human systems connect through space and time.

I spend much of my work using GIS and remote sensing to explore ecological patterns and support conservation decisions. I’m passionate about turning spatial data into meaningful environmental insights.

GIS & Research Experience

I have taken multiple GIS and remote sensing courses where I developed skills in spatial analysis, geospatial data processing, and cartographic design.

I have applied GIS in both undergraduate and graduate research to study ecological systems, map biodiversity patterns, and support conservation planning. My work includes analyzing satellite imagery, building spatial datasets, and producing maps for scientific interpretation.
