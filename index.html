Hi, I’m Maddie! I am a Master’s student at Temple University in Dr. Brent Sewall’s Lab. I’ve always felt most at home outdoors, whether hiking, climbing, or exploring ecosystems. That connection to nature has grown into a commitment to understanding and protecting biodiversity through ecological research and conservation.

GIS and Research Experience

I have taken multiple courses in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and remote sensing, where I developed strong technical skills in spatial analysis, mapping, and data visualization. Through these experiences, I worked with geospatial datasets, satellite imagery, and environmental data.

I have applied GIS in both my undergraduate and graduate research to analyze ecological patterns, map biodiversity, and support conservation-focused projects. My work integrates GIS tools to better understand environmental systems and contribute to data-driven conservation strategies.
